Based on ICHD-3 criteriaIntHeadacheSoc(IHS)+1 and clinical differentiation literature:
Headache Differentiation: Migraine vs TTH vs CGH
Feature	Migraine	TTH	CGH
Classification	Primary	Primary	Secondary (to cervical dysfunction)
Location	Unilateral (can shift)	Bilateral	Strictly unilateral, side-locked
Pain quality	Throbbing / pulsating	Pressing / tightening (band-like)	Dull, aching — starts in neck, radiates forward
Severity	Moderate–severe	Mild–moderate	Mild–moderate
Duration	4–72 hrs	30 min–7 days	Hours to days
Neck involvement	Can have neck pain (secondary)	Can have neck tension (secondary)	Neck movement provokes / reproduces it
Nausea/vomiting	Yes (often)	No	No
Photo/phonophobia	Both present	At most ONE of photo OR phono	Typically absent
Aura	Possible (visual, sensory, language)	No	No
Activity effect	Worsened by routine activity	Not aggravated	Variable
Autonomic features	Subtle (possible)	None	None
Key clinical test	—	—	Cervical AROM provokes headache; manual pressure on upper cervical joints reproduces pain
Key Differentiators at a Glance

Migraine over TTH:VADoDClinicalPracticeGuidelines+1

    Throbbing + moderate-severe + worsened by activity = migraine
    Both photo AND phonophobia = migraine (TTH only gets one)
    Nausea/vomiting = migraine, not TTH

CGH over migraine:PMRKnowledgeNow(AAPMR)+2

    Side-locked unilateral pain (migraine can alternate sides)
    Cervical movement or sustained posture triggers/reproduces the headache
    Digital pressure on upper cervical joints (C1–3) reproduces familiar pain — this is the most clinically useful sign
    No aura, no nausea, no autonomic features
    Reduced cervical ROM typically present

CGH over TTH:PMRKnowledgeNow(AAPMR)+1

    TTH is bilateral; CGH is strictly unilateral
    TTH has no neck mechanical provocation
    CGH has a clear posterior-to-anterior radiation pattern

Practical Note for Physio

The flexion-rotation test (FRT) is a useful physical test for CGH — restricted and/or symptom-reproducing rotation in full flexion implicates the C1–2 segment and has reasonable sensitivity/specificity for CGH.APTAOrthopedics It won't differentiate migraine from TTH, but it will help confirm a cervicogenic source when positive.
