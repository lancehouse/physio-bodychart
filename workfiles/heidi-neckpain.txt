Key Differentiators Clinically

Radiculopathy vs axial/facet pain:PubMedCentral(PMC)+1

    Arm pain worse than neck pain = strongly suggests radiculopathy
    Dermatomal pattern + at least one of: altered reflex, weakness, paraesthesia = radiculopathy cluster
    Spurling's + ULNT1 + distraction test — 3 of these positive together has good specificity for radiculopathyClinicalTrialsGov

Discogenic vs facet:PubMedCentral(PMC)+1

    Flexion loads the disc; extension loads the facet — use this as a screen
    Discogenic: deep, constant, often worse at night, flexion-dominant aggravation
    Facet: more localised, extension + rotation provokes, PA joint pressure reproduces familiar pain
    Note: referral patterns from disc and facet overlap significantly — Grubb & Kelly found shoulder referral from IVD mimics facet referralUeaeprints

Myelopathy — must not miss:PubMedCentral(PMC)

    Hand clumsiness, grip/release deterioration, gait instability, urinary urgency — even without significant neck pain
    Bilateral symptoms = red flag, requires urgent imaging
    Hyperreflexia, positive Hoffman's, positive Babinski = UMN, refer immediately

Red Flags Across All Categories

Worth a quick screen on every neck pain patient:APTAOrthopedics+1

    Bilateral arm symptoms — suspect myelopathy or central disc herniation
    Progressive neurological deficit — urgent referral
    Trauma + midline tenderness — Canadian C-Spine Rule / imaging before manual therapy
    Night pain + unexplained weight loss / age >50 — screen for malignancy
    Vertebrobasilar symptoms (drop attacks, diplopia, dizziness, dysarthria, dysphagia) — assess before cervical manipulation
