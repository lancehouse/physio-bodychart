### Physical / Nociceptive

- [ ] **Significant disease/pathology/physical factors — nociceptive pain type**
  → Carefully graded rehabilitation with pacing strategies

- [ ] **Significant pacing issues — "boom-bust" pattern**
  → Pacing education, problem solving and monitoring

- [ ] **Moderate severity inflammatory features**
  → Medication review and pacing strategies

- [ ] **Deconditioning** (>50% activity reduction for >3 months)
  → Goal-based graded activity and exercise

- [ ] **Significant regional reduction in passive movement / resistance**
  → Specific stretching regimen; full MSK screen required
  - Region/level::

- [ ] **Asymmetrical gait** — moderate severity due to pain and weakness
  → Identification and addressing of specific weakness (hip/knee). Low-load retraining with weight transfer progression to gait re-education and normalisation in ADL

- [ ] **Significant regional strength deficits**
  → Specific strengthening regimen
  - [ ] Gluteus maximus
  - [ ] Gluteus medius/minimus
  - [ ] Iliopsoas
  - [ ] Quadriceps
  - Other::

- [ ] **Reduced functional activation of deep/local/postural muscles**
  → Specific activation in low-load positions progressing to functional tasks
  - [ ] Lumbar multifidus
  - [ ] Transversus abdominis
  - [ ] Thoracic erector spinae
  - Other::

- [ ] **Significant overactivity of muscles**
  → Relaxation, stretching, breathing, then motor control retraining. Consider manual soft tissue techniques
  - [ ] Erector spinae
  - [ ] Quadratus lumborum
  - [ ] Rectus abdominis
  - [ ] External obliques
  - [ ] Piriformis
  - [ ] Iliopsoas
  - [ ] Hamstrings
  - [ ] Short hip adductors
  - Other::

- [ ] **Moderately increased nerve mechanosensitivity**
  → Neurodynamic mobilisation through exercise; consider manual mobilisation
  - Region::

- [ ] **Relevant diet and/or weight issues**
  → Dietitian advice in parallel with program

### Neuropathic

- [ ] **Moderate severity neuropathic pain** — with confirmed nerve injury on investigations
  → Education and medication review (discussion with treating doctor) for 4 weeks

- [ ] **Moderate severity neuropathic pain** — without confirmed nerve injury on investigations
  → Education and medication review (discussion with treating doctor) for 4 weeks

---

### Nociplastic / Central Sensitisation

- [ ] **Moderate severity nociplastic pain including central sensitisation**
  → Pain education. Graded activity/exercise (desensitisation focus). Strategies to increase positive emotions/descending inhibition. Consider medication review via GP liaison

- [ ] **Confirmed Complex Regional Pain Syndrome** (Budapest criteria)
  → Education, medication review, graded motor imagery, carefully graded activity

- [ ] **Functional neurological disorder**
  → Education, illustration of inconsistency between voluntary and automatic movement, graded motor imagery, carefully graded activity. Consider psychology/psychiatry referral

---

### Psychological

- [ ] **Mild/Moderate/Severe/Extremely severe depression** (DASS-21)
  → Pain and depression education, meaningful goal setting, weekly goals (behavioural activation), mindfulness
  - Severity:: [ ] Mild [ ] Moderate [ ] Severe [ ] Extremely severe
  - Psychiatry referral:: [ ] Yes [ ] No

- [ ] **Mild/Moderate/Severe/Extremely severe anxiety** (DASS-21)
  → Pain and anxiety education, breathing exercises, mindfulness, weekly goals focused on exposure (where appropriate)
  - Severity:: [ ] Mild [ ] Moderate [ ] Severe [ ] Extremely severe
  - Psychiatry referral:: [ ] Yes [ ] No

- [ ] **Mild/Moderate/Severe/Extremely severe stress** (DASS-21)
  → Pain and stress education, problem solving education/implementation, mindfulness
  - Severity:: [ ] Mild [ ] Moderate [ ] Severe [ ] Extremely severe
  - Psychiatry referral:: [ ] Yes [ ] No

- [ ] **Moderate pain catastrophising** (PCS + clinical assessment)
  → Pain education, catastrophising education, completion of weekly goals, mindfulness

- [ ] **Reduced pain self-efficacy** (PSEQ + clinical assessment)
  → Pain education, self-efficacy education, problem solving education, completion of weekly goals

- [ ] **Moderate unhelpful beliefs** impacting pain management
  → Pain education and cognitive behavioural approach
  - Beliefs present:
    - [ ] Unrealistic recovery expectations
    - [ ] Strong symptom focus
    - [ ] Strong cure focus
    - [ ] Desire for further treatment/investigations

- [ ] **PTSD-type symptoms** (PCL-5)
  → Pain and stress education. Review PTSD formally. Breathing techniques, mindfulness, exposure intervention if required
  - Mechanism:: [ ] Motor vehicle accident [ ] Traumatic work accident [ ] Other::
  - Psychiatry referral:: [ ] Yes [ ] No

- [ ] **Unclear readiness for change**
  → Formally review progress during program

### Sleep

- [ ] **Moderately disturbed sleep** due to pain and/or rumination
  → Testing of mechanisms behind sleep impairment, specific sleep intervention, sleep hygiene, medication review

---

### Social / Contextual

- [ ] **Moderate home/social barriers**
  → Communication education, problem solving, family review (if appropriate), goals focused on family/social engagement, financial counselling if indicated
  - Issues present:
    - [ ] Reduced family support
    - [ ] Reduced social support
    - [ ] Relationship issues (immediate family)
    - [ ] Personal relationship issues
    - [ ] Financial difficulties
    - [ ] Residential instability
    - [ ] Distance from program location

- [ ] **Moderate return to work barriers** — physical and psychosocial
  → RTW planning during program. Worksite assessment by team

---

### Medical / Systemic

- [ ] **Red flag — requires further investigation**
  → Further investigation and close monitoring
  - Flag::

- [ ] **Significant maladaptive use of prescription medication / non-prescription drugs / alcohol**
  → Medication review
  - Substance::

- [ ] **Possible ankylosing spondylitis**
  → Rheumatologist review via GP. Education. Carefully graded rehabilitation with pacing

- [ ] **Possible lumbar symptoms due to AAA**
  → Cardiologist review via GP

- [ ] **Possible vascular claudication**
  → Rheumatologist review via GP. Education. Graded rehabilitation. Address CV risk factors including dietitian services

- [ ] **Moderate severity cervical headache**
  → Further classification and specific treatment as required

- [ ] **Medico-legal/claim issues** impacting pain management
  → Formally review progress during program
