## Nociceptive Pain Features
<!-- reference: Smart et al 2010 -->
<!-- definition: pain arising from actual or threatened damage to non-neural tissue due to activation of nociceptors -->
<!-- ui-hint: show definition as collapsible panel. Tally subjective and examination features separately -->

### Subjective Features
- [ ] Clear, proportionate mechanical/anatomical nature to aggravating and easing factors
- [ ] Pain associated with and proportionate to trauma, pathological process, or movement/postural dysfunction
- [ ] Pain localised to area of injury/dysfunction (with/without somatic referral)
- [ ] Usually resolving or resolving in accordance with expected tissue healing times
- [ ] Responsive to simple analgesia / NSAIDs
- [ ] Absence of constant/unremitting pain
- [ ] Pain in association with other symptoms of inflammation
- [ ] Pain of recent onset

### Examination Features
- [ ] Clear, consistent, proportionate mechanical/anatomical pattern of pain reproduction on movement/mechanical testing
- [ ] Localised pain on palpation
- [ ] Absent or proportionate findings of hyperalgesia and/or allodynia
- [ ] Presence of antalgic postures/movement patterns

## Neuropathic Pain Features
<!-- reference: Smart et al 2010 -->
<!-- definition: pain caused by a lesion or disease of the somatosensory nervous system -->

### Subjective Features
- [ ] Burning, shooting, sharp, aching or electric shock-like quality
- [ ] History of nerve injury
- [ ] Neurological symptoms including paraesthesia
- [ ] Pain referred in dermatomal or cutaneous distribution
- [ ] Less responsive to simple analgesia/NSAIDs; more responsive to anti-epileptic/antidepressant medication
- [ ] High severity and irritability
- [ ] Mechanical pattern involving activities/postures loading or compressing neural tissue
- [ ] Other dysaesthesias (burning, coldness, crawling)
- [ ] Spontaneous (stimulus-independent) pain and/or paroxysmal pain

### Examination Features
- [ ] Symptom provocation with provocative neurodynamic tests
- [ ] Pain/symptom provocation on palpation of relevant neural tissues
- [ ] Positive neurological findings
- [ ] Antalgic posturing of affected limb/body part
- [ ] Positive hyperalgesia/allodynia within distribution of pain

## Nociplastic Pain Features
<!-- reference: IASP iasp-pain.org -->
<!-- definition: pain arising from altered nociception despite no clear evidence of nociceptive or neuropathic pain -->
<!-- ui-hint: show IASP definition as collapsible reference -->
<!-- note: potential mechanisms — central sensitisation, psychosocial factors, immune-related factors -->

### Subjective Features
- [ ] Disproportionate, non-mechanical, unpredictable pattern of provocation with multiple/non-specific factors
- [ ] Pain persisting beyond expected tissue healing/pathology recovery times
- [ ] Pain disproportionate to nature and extent of injury or pathology
- [ ] Widespread, non-anatomical distribution of pain
- [ ] History of failed interventions
- [ ] Strong association with unhelpful psychosocial factors (negative emotions, poor self-efficacy, unhelpful beliefs, altered family/work/social life, medical conflict)
- [ ] Unresponsive to NSAIDs; more responsive to anti-epileptic/antidepressant medication
- [ ] Spontaneous (stimulus-independent) pain and/or paroxysmal pain
- [ ] High levels of functional disability
- [ ] More constant/unremitting pain
- [ ] At least moderate night pain/disturbed sleep
- [ ] Other dysaesthesias (burning, coldness, crawling)
- [ ] High severity and irritability

### Examination Features
- [ ] Disproportionate, inconsistent, non-mechanical/non-anatomical pattern of pain provocation
- [ ] Positive secondary hyperalgesia/allodynia within distribution of pain
- [ ] Diffuse/non-anatomic areas of pain/tenderness on palpation
- [ ] Positive psychosocial factors (catastrophisation, fear-avoidance, distress)

Likelihood:: [ ] Low [ ] Moderate [ ] High
